How to Grade Pokemon Cards in 2026: PSA vs BGS vs CGC Complete Guide

Last updated: March 2026 • 14 min read

Quick Answer

To grade Pokemon cards, choose a grading company (PSA is the most popular for Pokemon), create an account on their website, fill out a submission form, place your cards in card savers, and ship them in. Costs start at $15-$20 per card with turnaround times from 2 business days to 120+ days depending on the service tier you choose.

Getting your Pokemon cards professionally graded is one of the most impactful things you can do to increase their value. A raw (ungraded) 1st Edition Base Set Charizard might sell for $5,000-$15,000, but a PSA 10 graded copy can fetch over $300,000. The grade provides authentication, condition verification, and a tamper-evident case that buyers trust.

But grading is not free, and not every card is worth grading. This guide covers everything you need to know: which company to choose, what the grades mean, how to prepare your submission, and whether the investment makes financial sense for your cards.

PSA (Professional Sports Authenticator)

PSA is the gold standard for Pokemon card grading. Their slabs are the most recognized, most liquid, and typically command the highest premiums on the secondary market. If you are grading Pokemon cards for resale, PSA is almost always the best choice. The downside is that their turnaround times have historically been unpredictable during high-demand periods.

BGS (Beckett Grading Services)

BGS offers the most detailed grading with four subgrades: Centering, Edges, Corners, and Surface. Each subgrade is scored individually, and the overall grade is a weighted average. The coveted BGS 10 "Black Label" (all four subgrades at 10) is the hardest top grade to achieve in the hobby. BGS is more popular in the sports card world but has a loyal following among Pokemon collectors who appreciate the subgrade detail.

CGC (Certified Guaranty Company)

CGC entered the trading card market more recently (they are established in comics grading). Their lower pricing and competitive turnaround times make them attractive for budget-conscious collectors. CGC grades carry slightly lower resale premiums than PSA, but the gap has been narrowing as the company builds reputation in the Pokemon space.

What Do the Grades Mean?

Understanding what each grade level represents helps you self-assess your cards before submission and set realistic expectations.

10

Gem Mint (PSA) / Pristine (BGS)

Virtually perfect. No visible flaws under magnification. Centering within 60/40 or better on front, 75/25 on back. Sharp corners, clean surfaces, perfect print quality. The highest achievable grade.

9

Mint

Near-perfect with only the slightest imperfection. May have one very minor flaw such as slightly off-center (within 65/35) or a tiny surface mark. Still an excellent grade that commands strong premiums.

8

Near Mint-Mint

Minor imperfections visible upon close inspection. Slight corner whitening, minor centering issues, or a small surface scratch. Cards that were well-stored but handled occasionally often fall here.

7

Near Mint

Some visible wear including light corner wear, minor edge whitening, or slight surface scratches. Cards that were played with lightly or stored without sleeves often grade here.

5-6

Excellent to Excellent-Mint

Noticeable wear including corner rounding, edge wear, surface scratches, or moderate off-centering. Cards that were played with regularly but not abused typically fall in this range.

1-4

Poor to Very Good-Excellent

Significant wear, creases, tears, staining, or heavy damage. Even at low grades, extremely rare cards (like Pikachu Illustrator) can still hold substantial value due to scarcity.

Step-by-Step: How to Submit Cards for Grading

Step 1

Self-Assess Your Cards

Before spending money on grading, examine each card carefully. Use a jeweler's loupe or magnifying glass to check for surface scratches, edge whitening, corner damage, and print defects. Only submit cards you believe will grade 8 or higher, unless the card is valuable enough at lower grades to justify the cost.

Step 2

Create an Account

Go to the grading company's website (psacard.com, beckett.com, or cgccomics.com) and create a free account. You will need this to fill out submission forms, track your order, and receive results.

Step 3

Choose Your Service Level

Select a tier based on your budget and urgency. Economy tiers ($15-$20) take months but are the most cost-effective for bulk submissions. Express tiers ($100-$200) offer 10-20 business day turnaround. For high-value cards, consider insuring the shipment and choosing a faster tier.

Step 4

Fill Out the Submission Form

Enter each card's details: set name, card number, year, and declared value. The declared value affects the maximum coverage if the card is lost or damaged. Be accurate — declaring a significantly wrong value can cause issues.

Step 5

Prepare Your Cards for Shipping

This step is critical. Place each card in a penny sleeve first, then slide it into a Card Saver I (semi-rigid holder). PSA specifically requires Card Saver I holders — do not use top loaders for PSA submissions. Write the line number from your submission form on the Card Saver with a marker.

Step 6

Pack and Ship

Place the Card Savers in a snug box with padding. Use painter's tape to secure the cards in place — do not let them shift during transit. Ship with tracking and insurance matching your declared value. USPS Priority Mail with insurance or UPS are common choices.

Step 7

Track and Receive

Monitor your submission status through the grading company's website. Once graded, cards are returned in tamper-evident slabs. Verify each grade and check the certification number online to confirm it matches your card.

Is Grading Worth It? The Math

Grading is an investment, so you should calculate whether the expected value increase justifies the cost. Here is a framework:

When Grading is Worth It

  • The card's raw value exceeds 5x the grading cost. If grading costs $20, the card should be worth at least $100 raw.
  • You believe the card will grade 9 or 10. The price jump from 8 to 10 can be 5x-50x on popular cards.
  • The card has strong market demand. Charizard, Pikachu, Eeveelutions, and other popular Pokemon see the biggest grade premiums.
  • You plan to sell or insure the card. Graded cards are easier to sell and verify for insurance purposes.

When Grading is Not Worth It

  • The card is worth under $20 raw. Even a PSA 10 on a $5 card might only be worth $20-$30 — not enough to justify costs.
  • The card has visible damage. If you can see corner wear, creases, or scratches with the naked eye, the card likely will not grade high enough to justify the cost.
  • You are grading for personal collection. If you never plan to sell, consider whether the grading cost is worth the display upgrade. For many collectors, a quality one-touch magnetic case achieves a similar display effect for $3-$5.

Pro Tips for Higher Grades

  1. Sleeve immediately after pulling. Every second a card is exposed to handling is a chance for damage. Use perfect-fit sleeves for the tightest protection.
  2. Check centering first. Centering is the most common reason for a grade reduction. Use a centering tool or app to measure before submitting.
  3. Inspect under bright, angled light. Surface scratches and print lines become visible when light hits the card at an angle.
  4. Handle by edges only. Fingerprint oils can leave marks that affect the surface grade. Consider wearing cotton gloves for high-value cards.
  5. Store in a cool, dry environment. Humidity warps cards and can cause surface damage. Avoid attics, basements, and garages.
  6. Do not clean or alter cards. Attempting to remove dirt, erase marks, or trim edges is considered alteration and will result in the card being flagged or receiving an "Authentic" designation without a numeric grade.

Common Grading Mistakes to Avoid

Submitting too many low-value cards. It is tempting to grade everything, but grading fees add up. Focus on cards where the grade significantly impacts value.

Using the wrong holders. PSA requires Card Saver I holders. Sending cards in top loaders or other holders can delay processing or result in returned submissions.

Not insuring your shipment. If your package is lost or damaged, you want coverage. Match the insurance to the declared value of your submission.

Expecting PSA 10 on every card. Statistically, many submissions receive 8s and 9s. Set realistic expectations and do not over-invest in grading fees.

Forgetting to check pop reports. Before submitting, check the population report on PSA's website. If there are already thousands of PSA 10 copies, the grade premium may be smaller than expected.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to grade a Pokemon card in 2026?

PSA grading starts at $20/card for their Economy tier (estimated 120+ business day turnaround). BGS starts at $20/card for Standard. CGC starts at $15/card for Economy. Express and premium services cost $50-$300+ per card depending on turnaround speed and declared value.

Is PSA or BGS better for Pokemon cards?

PSA is the most recognized grading service for Pokemon cards and typically commands higher resale premiums. BGS is preferred by some collectors for its sub-grade breakdown (centering, edges, corners, surface). For maximum resale value on Pokemon specifically, PSA is generally the better choice.

What does PSA 10 mean?

PSA 10 (Gem Mint) means the card is virtually perfect with sharp corners, perfect centering (60/40 or better on front), no surface scratches visible under magnification, no whitening on edges, and flawless print quality. It is the highest grade PSA awards.

How long does PSA grading take in 2026?

PSA turnaround times in 2026 vary by tier: Economy (120+ business days, ~$20/card), Regular (65 business days, ~$50/card), Express (20 business days, ~$100/card), Super Express (10 business days, ~$200/card), and Walk-Through (2 business days, $300+/card). Times are estimates and can vary.

Is it worth grading modern Pokemon cards?

It depends on the card. For chase cards worth $50+ raw (like illustration rares, special art rares, or gold cards), grading can be worthwhile if you believe the card will grade PSA 10. For bulk cards worth under $20, the grading cost likely exceeds the value increase.

Can I grade cards at a card show?

Yes, PSA, BGS, and CGC all attend major card shows and conventions where they accept on-site submissions. This can be convenient because you skip shipping costs, but pricing is typically at the standard or higher tiers. Check each company's event calendar for upcoming shows.
